Education

Anxiety Disorders: These include conditions such as generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and specific phobias. Anxiety disorders involve excessive worry, fear, or apprehension that can interfere with daily life.

Mood Disorders: Mood disorders affect a person's emotional state. Major depressive disorder (depression) is characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities. Bipolar disorder involves alternating periods of depression and mania.

Psychotic Disorders: Psychotic disorders involve disruptions to a person's thoughts and perceptions, leading to difficulties in distinguishing between what is real and what is not. Schizophrenia is one example of a psychotic disorder, marked by symptoms such as hallucinations and delusions.

Trauma-Related Disorders: Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and acute stress disorder are examples of conditions that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Symptoms may include intrusive memories, flashbacks, avoidance of triggers, and heightened arousal.
